#  Customer Churn Analysis — Business-Focused Case Study

##  Overview
Acquiring new customers is 5-25x more expensive than retaining existing ones. This project investigates a dataset of **594,000+ customers** to pinpoint the drivers of attrition and deliver a prioritized retention roadmap for stakeholders.

---

##  Objective
- Identify the primary drivers of customer churn  
- Analyze customer behavior patterns across different segments  
- Generate actionable business insights to reduce churn rate  

---

##  Tech Stack & Methodology
* **Data Manipulation:** `Pandas`, `NumPy` (Feature engineering, missing value imputation).
* **Visualization:** `Matplotlib`, `Seaborn` (Correlation heatmaps, distribution plots).
* **Advanced Analytics:** Entropy-based variability analysis and skewness checks for distribution normality.

---

##  Dataset
- Telecom Customer Churn Dataset (Kaggle)  
- Includes customer demographics, service usage, billing information, and churn status

##  Key Insights

###  1. Tenure is a Strong Predictor of Churn
- Customers with low tenure show significantly higher churn rates  
- Indicates early-stage dissatisfaction or onboarding issues  

 **Business Insight:**  
Focus on improving the first 3–6 months of customer experience  

---

### 2. Contract Type Strongly Impacts Retention
- Month-to-month contract users exhibit the highest churn  
- Long-term contract users are more stable  

 **Business Insight:**  
Encourage long-term subscriptions through incentives  

---

###  3. Monthly Charges and Churn Relationship
- Higher monthly charges are associated with increased churn probability  

 **Business Insight:**  
Customers may perceive low value for high cost  

---

###  4. Service Usage Patterns Influence Churn
- Customers with fewer engaged services are more likely to churn  

**Business Insight:**  
Higher engagement → lower churn risk  

---

###  5. Customer Segmentation Opportunity
- Clear high-risk groups identified:
  - Low tenure + high charges  
  - Month-to-month contract users  

 **Business Insight:**  
Targeted retention strategies can be applied  

---

##  Key Takeaway
This project demonstrates how data analysis can move beyond visualization to deliver **actionable business insights** that directly impact customer retention and profitability.
